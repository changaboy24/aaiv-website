  • Tomorrow Magazine

    Me and my good friend Renee Solorzano built this site for our friends and former co-workers. It's a beautiful magazine, thanks to Renee, and filled with awesome, interesting and informative content.

  • Take Back Tuesday

    Made this for my work with GOOD, with Keith Bormuth and Casey Caplowe. It's a beautiful site, and an awesome campaign. It's all about getting employers to give their employees the day off on election day so they can vote. I'm all for it!

  • I Would Bang You

    It's a dating site that I created by myself in a stroke of 'brilliance'. It's pretty straightforward, if you say you would bang someone, and they say they would bang you, then you can message each other. It's one of my favorite designs.

  • Sortr

    It's a little contact sorting and tagging application that I created at GOOD to allow people to add themselves to email groups. But it could totally be useful for others.

  • Just a 'lil bit about Ryan MacInnes...

    I made my very first website when I was 14. It was absolutely terrible. I've been making websites ever since, some of which are slightly better. I doubled majored in Environmental Biology and Microbiology in college, which has done exactly zero for me career-wise. Once I graduated I moved to LA and got back into making websites. I started working in Rails in late 2007. In 2009 I started working for Sony Pictures as a project manager, making websites freelance on the side. In early 2012 I started working at GOOD as a software engineer. I also moved to New York City. Now I work in it almost exclusively, along with HTML5, CSS3, Javascript (jQuery of course) with a litle bit of Backbone all using TDD. I'm also not bad in Python, Bash and I'm teaching myself C at the the moment. I'm the best at creating your MVP, making your idea into a reality, as cliche as that sounds. I'm an okay designer, I like photoshop and not illustrator. I love learning new things and I love taking on challenges. I especially love getting paid to do both. So do it already!
